Key Topics

Social Engineering

Social engineering is a type of cyberattack that relies on manipulating people rather than hacking systems. Attackers may use fake emails, messages, or websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or personal data. Being cautious and verifying sources can help prevent these attacks.

Passwords

Passwords are the first line of defense for protecting your online accounts. Weak or reused passwords make it easier for attackers to gain access to your information. Using strong, unique passwords and enabling additional security measures like multi-factor authentication can greatly improve your security.

Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to harm or exploit devices and networks. It can take many forms, such as viruses, ransomware, or spyware, and can steal data, damage systems, or monitor user activity. Keeping software updated and avoiding suspicious downloads can help protect against malware.
